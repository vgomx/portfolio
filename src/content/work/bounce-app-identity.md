overview: "Bounce grew out of Darisni's ecosystem as a dedicated tool for nurseries: a platform where parents follow their children's day through announcements, events, payments, and direct messaging with teachers. The visual identity had to work at every scale — from a 60-pixel icon on a parent's home screen to full-bleed marketing collateral — while staying clearly connected to Darisni's existing brand. The result is an energetic system that borrows the parent brand's confidence and adds its own character: lime, teal, and fuchsia, anchored by a mark that doubles as both a logotype letter and a small jumping figure."
challenge: "Designing for children without infantilising the product. The app is used by parents and nursery staff — professional operators who need to trust the platform — but it exists in the world of childhood, play, and care. The colour palette had to be warm and energetic without tipping into noise. The mark itself needed to resolve two things at once: reading as a child-like figure with arms raised while also functioning as a custom letterform inside the word 'bounce'. The identity also had to carry cultural specificity for the Gulf context it was built for, using patterns and visual language that felt regionally rooted rather than generic."
processIntro: "The project started with the mark and worked outward. Once the logotype was locked — with the figure embedded in the 'o' — the colour system followed, establishing three voices: teal as the primary tone, lime as the energetic accent, and fuchsia as the surprise. Pattern language came next, derived directly from the mark's geometry and tiled into a repeating motif that carries across app backgrounds, onboarding screens, and brand collateral."
