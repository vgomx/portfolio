overview: "Banco Original is a Brazilian digital bank with a growing customer base but a widening gap against competitors — fewer features, weaker customer support, and little to keep users from splitting their financial lives across multiple accounts. The central question: what would it take for Original to become customers' primary bank? This project, developed through a full design thinking process, resulted in Gerente Original — a virtual banking manager concept that brings personalised guidance, smarter service delivery, and specialist investment support directly into the app."
challenge: "Research started with desk research and competitive benchmarking across Brazil's major digital banks. The findings were clear: fee exemption had become a commodity, and insurance, loans, and rewards programmes were now standard. The one area still lagging was service quality — specifically, the lack of a human or intelligent touchpoint for customers who needed help. A CSD matrix helped structure the open questions; a ten-question survey validated the assumptions. Interviews brought the frustration into focus: users described long waits for dispute resolution, impersonal bot interactions, and the absence of specialist support for investments. Four hypotheses emerged from synthesis — expanding the service portfolio, improving chat response times, introducing AI voice assistance, and creating a virtual manager. A prioritisation matrix then narrowed the focus to the hypothesis with the highest user impact and clearest path to implementation: Gerente Original."
